Homemade Churros

By John Simpson September 10, 2025

These homemade churros bring back memories of warm summer evenings at the local fair. Crispy on the outside, soft and tender on the inside, and coated with cinnamon sugar, they're the perfect treat for any celebration or special occasion. Serve them with a rich chocolate dipping sauce for an authentic Spanish-inspired dessert that everyone will love.

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 35 minutes
Servings: 4-6

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (for coating)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (for coating)

For the Chocolate Dipping Sauce

  • 4 ounces dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine water, butter, salt, and 1 tablespoon sugar. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Once the mixture is boiling and the butter has melted completely, reduce heat to low and add the flour all at once.
  3. Stir vigorously with a wooden spoon until the mixture forms a ball and pulls away from the sides of the pan, about 1 minute.
  4. Remove from heat and let the dough cool for 5 minutes.
  5. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition until the dough is smooth and glossy.
  6. Transfer the dough to a piping bag fitted with a large star tip (1/2-inch diameter).
  7. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C).
  8. Carefully pipe 4-5 inch strips of dough directly into the hot oil, cutting the end with scissors. Work in batches of 3-4 churros at a time to avoid overcrowding.
  9. Fry until golden brown, turning occasionally, about 2-3 minutes per batch.
  10. Use a slotted spoon to remove churros and drain on paper towels.
  11. In a shallow dish, mix 1/2 cup sugar with cinnamon. Roll the warm churros in the cinnamon-sugar mixture until coated.
  12. For the chocolate sauce: Heat heavy cream in a small saucepan until it just begins to simmer. Remove from heat and add chopped chocolate. Let stand for 1 minute, then stir until smooth. Add vanilla and salt.
  13. Serve churros warm with chocolate dipping sauce.

Notes

For best results, make sure the oil temperature stays around 350°F (175°C). If the oil is too hot, the churros will brown too quickly without cooking inside. If it's too cool, they'll absorb too much oil and become greasy.

The dough can be made up to 2 hours ahead and kept at room temperature in the piping bag. If the dough becomes too stiff, warm it slightly by placing the piping bag in warm water for a few seconds.

For a different flavor, try adding a teaspoon of vanilla or a pinch of nutmeg to the dough.
