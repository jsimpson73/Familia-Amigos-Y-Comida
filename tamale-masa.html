Tamale Masa

By John Simpson September 15, 2025

Tamale masa can be a touchy topic in certain circles of people. Everyone that makes tamales has their own perspective. I know, most tamales do not have much flavor in the masa. In my humble opinion, that makes the best of those tamales, okay.If you want family and friends to really enjoy and anticipate your tamales, you have to infuse flavor into the masa as well as whatever filling you plan to use.

This makes a good tamale a GOOD tamale!

Prep Time: Too long
Cook Time: 60 minutes
Total Time: All Day
Servings: 11 to 13 dozen

Ingredients

  • 1.5 gallon hot water/broth
  • 1 bag (16.5 cups) masa
  • 1 cups oil
  • 1/3 cup chili powder
  • 1/3 cup garlic powder
  • 1/3 cup salt

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine chili powder, garlic, and salt into masa.
  2. Add oil and knead thoroughly. Pour water/broth in bowl. Knead thoroughly.
  3. Press a spoonful of masa onto husks with corn tortilla press.
  4. To spread with a spoon on husks, use a little more water/broth if masa is too thick and dry.
  5. *I use this masa. *I avoid the one that has Tamal on the label. I find it way too coarsely milled.

Instructions for use of a tortilla press

  1. Prepare your corn tortilla press by covering the top of press with a plastic shopping bag. Cut a piece of parchment paper to fit across the bottom of the tortilla press.
  2. Soak corn husks in warm water for @30 minutes.
  3. Remove some husks from the water and select some that are @ 4 ½ to 6 inches wide, at the bottom. Wipe dry with towel. Stack up as many as you want to have ready for masa.
  4. Place a serving spoon of masa on corn husk, smooth side up, and close tortilla press. Remove access masa, set aside and repeat, and repeat, and repeat, and repeat…
  5. Fill and roll up tamales. Place in a steamer and steam for 1 hour or until the husk peels from the edge of the tamale clean, when tested.
  6. Remove tamales from steamer and place on a cooling rack.
  7. Let tamales rest for at least 15 minutes so masa can set.

Notes

Seasoning the masa is one of the key reasons many family and friends love Vicky's popper tamales. Just ask Fabian & Rey. 😄
