Homemade Salsa

By Carlos Mendez August 20, 2025

This fresh and vibrant homemade salsa has been a staple in my family for generations. Made with ripe tomatoes, onions, cilantro, and a hint of lime, it's bursting with authentic flavors that store-bought versions simply can't match. The beauty of this recipe is its simplicity and versatility - you can adjust the heat level to your preference and serve it with tortilla chips, tacos, grilled meats, or even scrambled eggs. Once you try this homemade version, you'll never go back to jarred salsa again!

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Rest Time: 30 minutes
Total Time: 45 minutes
Servings: 8 (about 2 cups)

Ingredients

  • 4 ripe tomatoes, diced (about 2 cups)
  • 1/2 medium white onion, finely diced
  • 1-2 jalapeño or serrano peppers, seeded and minced (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 1 lime)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano (preferably Mexican)
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, onion, jalapeño or serrano peppers, and cilantro.
  2. Add the minced garlic, lime juice, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. If using, drizzle with olive oil and stir gently to combine all ingredients.
  4. Taste and adjust seasonings as needed, adding more salt, lime juice, or peppers according to your preference.
  5. For best flavor, cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  6. Before serving, stir again and taste, adjusting seasonings if necessary.
  7. Serve with tortilla chips, as a topping for tacos, or as a condiment for grilled meats.

Notes

For Chunky Salsa: Hand-chop all ingredients to your desired consistency.

For Smoother Salsa: Pulse ingredients briefly in a food processor, being careful not to over-process.

Heat Level: For mild salsa, remove all seeds and membranes from the peppers. For medium heat, leave some seeds. For hot salsa, use the entire pepper including seeds and membranes.

Variations:

  • Roasted Salsa: Char tomatoes, onions, and peppers under the broiler or on a grill before chopping for a smoky flavor.
  • Fruity Salsa: Add 1/2 cup diced mango, pineapple, or peach for a sweet-spicy variation.
  • Black Bean Salsa: Add 1 cup rinsed and drained black beans and 1/2 cup corn kernels.

Storage: Homemade salsa will keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. The flavors often improve after the first day.
