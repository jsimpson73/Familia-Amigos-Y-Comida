Applesauce & Apple Butter

By John Simpson September 15, 2025

This recipe is as close to my grandpa's as I can get. I have fond memories of the apple butter that my grandpa made. He passed when I was in middle school. Many of his recipes have been lost to time. Store bought just doesn't cut it. I had to try to replicate the best I had ever tasted.

Prep Time: 45 minutes
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Total Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
Servings: 6

Ingredients

Applesauce

  • 3 lbs apples, quartered and cored
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • sugar to taste

Apple Butter

  • 8 cups applesauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 2 cups brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • grated rind of 1 lemon
  • grated ring of 1\2 to 1 whole orange

Instructions

  1. Place apples in a saucepan with ½ cup water. Bring slowly to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until soft (about 10 minutes).
  2. Add cinnamon and sugar and stir.
  3. Pack hot applesauce into jars, allowing ½ inch headroom. Insert knife to pierce any air bubbles. Adjust lids.
  4. For long-term storage, process in boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  5. Makes 1 quart
  1. Mix all ingredients and spread in shallow baking pan.
  2. Bake at 275 degrees, stirring every 30 minutes, until thick (about four to six hours).
  3. Pack into hot, sterile canning jars.
  4. For long-term storage, process in boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  5. Makes 2 pints

Notes

Home made applesauce makes the best apple butter. Store bought can be used, just know that it is usually more watery, and will require longer bake times to get the apple butter to thicken properly. It should be spreadable, similar to jelly or preserves.
