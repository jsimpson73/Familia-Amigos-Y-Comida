Sunday Paella

By John Simpson September 5, 2025

This vibrant and flavorful paella has become our family's Sunday tradition. Inspired by the classic Spanish dish, it brings together a colorful array of ingredients in one spectacular pan. The saffron-infused rice, tender seafood, and savory chorizo create a dish that's perfect for gathering family and friends around the table. While traditional paella is cooked over an open fire, this version is adapted for the home kitchen while still capturing the essence of this beloved Spanish classic.

Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Total Time: 1 hour 15 minutes
Servings: 6-8

Ingredients

  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1/4 cup hot water
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 8 oz Spanish chorizo, sliced
  • 2 cups Bomba or Arborio rice
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lb mussels, cleaned
  • 8 oz calamari rings
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 lemons, cut into wedges
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, crush the saffron threads into the hot water and let steep for 15 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, warm the chicken broth over low heat.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large paella pan or wide, shallow skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add the garlic and bell peppers and cook for another 3 minutes.
  6. Add the chorizo and cook until it releases its oils and begins to crisp, about 5 minutes.
  7. Stir in the rice, coating it with the oils in the pan.
  8. Add the saffron mixture, paprika, oregano, and bay leaf. Stir to combine.
  9. Pour in the diced tomatoes and 5 cups of the warm chicken broth. Stir gently to distribute the ingredients evenly.
  10. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer. Do not stir the rice once it begins to simmer.
  11. Cook uncovered for about 15 minutes, or until the rice has absorbed most of the liquid but is still slightly firm.
  12. Scatter the peas over the rice.
  13. Arrange the shrimp, mussels (hinge side down), and calamari on top of the rice.
  14. Add the remaining cup of broth if the mixture seems dry.
  15. Cover with foil and cook for another 10-15 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the seafood is cooked through. The mussels should open (discard any that remain closed).
  16. Remove from heat and let stand, covered, for 5 minutes.
  17. Garnish with chopped parsley and lemon wedges.
  18. Bring the entire pan to the table for a dramatic presentation, and serve hot.

Notes

For authentic paella, try to find Spanish Bomba rice, which absorbs more liquid while maintaining its shape. Arborio rice is a good substitute if Bomba is unavailable.

The key to good paella is developing the socarrat, a caramelized crust of rice at the bottom of the pan. To achieve this, increase the heat slightly during the last few minutes of cooking, being careful not to burn the rice.

Feel free to customize the seafood based on what's fresh and available. You can also add chicken thighs (browned before adding the rice) for a mixed paella.

A traditional paella pan (paellera) is ideal, but any wide, shallow skillet will work.
